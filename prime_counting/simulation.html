Trivia

The distribution of prime numbers has been of central interest to mathematicians, purely due to recreational reasons. The prime counting function (often denoted as π(x)) has been numerically graphed here. As the limit tends to infinite, it is observed that π(x) takes on an increasinsly linear form. Several logarithmic functions and integrals are known to approximate it. It is well-known that the sequence of prime numbers is infinite, merely because the number obtained by adding 1 to the product of known primes is a prime number (due to its very method of construction).
